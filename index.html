I am a Ph.D. student in Biomedical Sciences at The Catholic University of Korea, specializing in cancer genomics, single-cell genomics, and bioinformatics. My research focuses on understanding the genetic underpinnings of cancer and developing computational tools for complex biological data analysis.

With a strong background in multi-omics data integration and machine learning approaches, I aim to contribute to precision oncology and personalized therapeutic strategies.

Liver Cancer Biomarker Discovery

Identified pseudogene-derived lncRNA biomarkers in liver cancer using TCGA-LIHC dataset (369 tumor, 49 normal tissues). Discovered 465 differentially expressed pseudogenes and identified PLEKHA8P1 as a key biomarker.

Collaboration: Prof. Lark Kyun Kim (Yonsei University College of Medicine)

Metachronous Gastric Cancer

Analyzed RNA-seq data from 46 patients, identified 13 DEGs including KDF1 and CDK1 as significant biomarkers for metachronous gastric cancer progression.

Collaboration: Prof. Soo-jeong Cho (Seoul National University College of Medicine)

Hematopoietic Stem Cell Niche

Analyzed transcriptional heterogeneity in HPSC using scRNA-seq data from Cxcr4 cKO and control mice. Identified key regulators of HSC homeostasis.

Collaboration: Prof. Joao Pereira (Yale University)
